Beginner → 1200 Elo
in 90 Days

A 12-week execution-ready sprint system. Every action links to a specific risk it eliminates. No passive learning — only measurable improvement.

12 Weeks · 3–4 actions each
1–2 hrs/day rapid time controls

Core Coaching Principles
01
Tactics before everything
90% of games below 1200 are decided by one-move blunders. Pattern recognition eats opening theory for breakfast.
02
Analyse every loss
A loss un-analysed is a lesson wasted. Use engine analysis to find your moment of failure — not just the blunder.
03
Simple systems only
One opening per colour. You play the same plans again and again. Understanding > memorisation at this level.
04
Measure ruthlessly
Track puzzle accuracy weekly. If it's not going up, the method is wrong. Adjust immediately.
05
Tilt is an Elo leak
Walk away after 2 consecutive losses. Revenge games are -20 Elo average. Log tilt incidents and patterns.

▶
♟
Blunder-check drill: Before every move, ask "Can my opponent take anything for free?"
Skill Gap

📊 Output: Track % of games with zero hanging pieces. Target: 50% by end of week.

📅
Set a fixed 60-min daily session slot. Write it in a calendar and protect it like a meeting.
Discipline

📊 Output: 7-day streak completed. Zero missed days in Week 1.

Playing without reflection cements bad habits. The 5-minute post-game reflection loop converts losses into lessons immediately, before memory fades.

WK 02
Opening Foundation: Two Systems, One Each Side
Install e4 (White) and Caro-Kann/e5 (Black). Principles only — no 15-move theory.
▶
📖
Study the 3 opening principles (30 min video or article): centre control, piece development, king safety.
Skill Gap
Below 1200, almost every opening problem is a violation of one of three principles. Understanding these makes your opening play self-correcting, even in unfamiliar positions.
📊 Output: Can explain all 3 principles from memory. Apply in next 5 games — track principle violations.
♔
Learn your White repertoire: 1.e4, castle kingside quickly, control the centre. Study via 15-min video.
Skill Gap
Simplicity is the goal. A system you understand beats a complex theory you've memorised. After 1.e4, focus on: develop knights before bishops, castle by move 8, don't move the same piece twice.
📊 Output: Castle by move 8 in ≥80% of White games this week.
♚
Choose and study one Black defence vs 1.e4 — recommend e5 (Open Game) for counterplay, or Caro-Kann for solid play.
Skill Gap
Pick one and stick to it for all 90 days. Familiarity with your own positions is worth more than theoretical breadth. The Caro-Kann leads to easier endgames; 1...e5 sharpens tactics faster.
📊 Output: Decision made. Opening played in ≥5 Black games. Record how often you reach a comfortable middlegame.
⛔
Tilt Protocol: After 2 consecutive losses, close the board for 30 minutes. Log the reason each time.
Tilt
Tilt is a silent Elo drain. The 2-loss rule is a hard stop that prevents emotional variance from compounding. Logging reasons turns tilt into data you can act on.
📊 Output: Tilt log started. Track # of tilt breaks taken. Goal: implement the protocol 100% of the time it triggers.

WK 03
Tactical Patterns: Forks & Simple Combinations
Learn to see knight forks, queen forks, and pawn forks before they happen.
▶
🔱
Puzzle theme: Fork only (Lichess Puzzle Storm or thematic set). 20 fork puzzles per day, 3 days.
Passive Learning
Thematic drilling burns patterns into fast-recognition memory. Forks are the most common tactical win below 1200. Seeing a fork opportunity takes <1 second once the pattern is wired.
📊 Output: Fork puzzle accuracy ≥75%. Can spot undefended fork squares without the clock.
🔍
First structured game analysis: Run 3 of your games through engine. Find the "losing move" in each. Write one sentence about why.
Passive Learning
The engine isn't for looking at all 30 moves — only for finding the moment the game turned. That single move, analysed deeply, teaches more than a complete game review.
📊 Output: 3 losing moves identified and logged. Pattern: were they all hanging pieces, or calculation errors?
📈
Weekly rating check: Record current Elo on your platform. Compare to Day 1 baseline. Note the delta.
Plateau
Early rating swings are noisy, but tracking keeps you honest. If rating is still below baseline after 3 weeks, the training method — not effort — needs to change.
📊 Output: Rating chart started (spreadsheet or notebook). First 3 data points logged.
🎯
Play 5 games with a specific intention: "I will not hang any piece in these 5 games." Track success rate.
Discipline
Intentional practice creates feedback loops. Playing with a specific goal — not just "trying to win" — forces the analytical mindset that will drive your rating up.
📊 Output: Piece-hanging rate recorded for all 5 games. Target: ≤1 per game on average.

Tactical Depth: Pins & Skewers
Add two new pattern families. Increase puzzle difficulty to 800–1000 Elo range.
▶
📌
Thematic drilling: 15 pin puzzles + 15 skewer puzzles on days 1–3. No mixed sets yet.
Passive Learning
Pins and skewers are the next most common tactical motifs after hanging pieces and forks. Isolating them first before mixing builds cleaner, faster recognition than doing random tactical puzzles.
📊 Output: Pin accuracy ≥78%. Skewer accuracy ≥75%. Written record of at least 2 patterns that surprised you.
🎲
Mixed puzzle sessions: Days 4–7, do 25 random-theme puzzles. Track accuracy against themed drilling baseline.
Skill Gap
The board doesn't announce tactics. Random drilling tests pattern transfer — the ability to spot a pin in a real game without a thematic hint. The gap between themed and random accuracy is your real skill gap.
📊 Output: Random accuracy ≥70%. If <10% gap vs themed, transfer is working. If >20% gap, redo themed drilling next week.
🕐
Time management drill: Play 5 games and track clock — never let your time fall below 20% unless opponent's is also low.
Skill Gap
Time trouble causes blunders independently of tactical skill. Many players below 1200 lose on time or blunder in time pressure. Tracking clock usage forces disciplined pace.
📊 Output: Average time remaining at game end logged for 5 games. Target: >20% always.
📝
Analyse 2 wins this week, not just losses. Find the move that gave you the advantage.
Passive Learning
Analysing wins reinforces what works and builds strategic confidence. Most players only analyse losses, which creates a negative feedback bias and ignores positive pattern reinforcement.
📊 Output: 2 winning moves identified. Can you recreate the thinking? Write it out in a sentence.

WK 06
Endgame Fundamentals: King & Pawn Endings
K+P vs K, opposition, the square rule. 20% of games below 1200 are won or lost in K+P endings.
▶
👑
Study the 3 essential K+P endings: opposition, the "square rule," and rook pawns. 20-min video + practice each day.
Skill Gap
K+P endgames are completely solvable — they're 100% calculable. Knowing opposition and the rule of the square converts drawn endgames to wins and lost endgames to draws immediately.
📊 Output: Can solve K+P vs K from any standard position correctly 9/10 times in Lichess endgame trainer.
🏁
Endgame trainer sessions: 15 min/day on Lichess endgame trainer, K+P themes only.
Passive Learning
Theory without practice doesn't stick. The Lichess endgame trainer provides immediate feedback and forces you to play out the technique, not just recognise it. This is active, not passive, learning.
📊 Output: 7 sessions completed. Accuracy tracked and improving. At least one endgame win in a real game this week.
⬆️
Increase puzzle difficulty: Move to 900–1100 Elo range. Expect accuracy to drop — that's correct.
Plateau
Staying in the comfort zone of easy puzzles will plateau your rating. Discomfort in puzzles directly translates to stronger positions and better calculation in games. A temporary accuracy dip is a growth signal.
📊 Output: New accuracy floor logged at harder difficulty. Target: stabilise ≥68% within 2 weeks at this level.
🧱
Identify your middlegame weakness: Do you lose material to tactics, or lose strategically by drifting? Engine analysis of 3 games.
Plateau
Below 1200, almost all defeats are tactical — but knowing if your problem is "I don't see tactics" vs "I drift into passive positions" tells you exactly where to direct training effort in Phase 3.
📊 Output: Weakness category named: Tactical (blunders) or Positional (drift). Phase 3 adjusted accordingly.

WK 07
Calculation Training & Candidate Moves
Learn to calculate 2–3 moves deep before every critical decision. Build the "candidate move" habit.
▶
🧠
Candidate move protocol: Before any critical move, list 2–3 candidate moves out loud (or in your head). Pick the best. Track in games.
Skill Gap
Most blunders happen when players commit to the first move they see. The candidate move habit inserts a mental pause that catches 70% of tactical blunders before they happen.
📊 Output: Applied in ≥80% of decisive moments in 5 games. Blunder rate in those games vs. previous baseline.
🔭
Calculation drill (no board): From a described position, calculate 3 moves ahead in your head. 10 positions, 3×/week.
Skill Gap
Visualisation is the bottleneck for most club-level players. Board vision — the ability to see future positions without moving pieces — is trainable and is directly responsible for calculation accuracy in games.
📊 Output: 3 sessions completed. Accuracy on 3-move calculations ≥60%. Improvement tracked week-over-week.
📊
Mid-programme rating check: Record Elo. Are you on pace for 1200? If not, identify the single biggest bottleneck.
Plateau
Week 7 is the critical feedback point. If rating hasn't moved meaningfully, the programme needs adjustment — not just more effort. A specific, honest bottleneck diagnosis is worth more than motivation.
📊 Output: Rating delta from baseline noted. If <50 points improvement, specific intervention plan written for Weeks 8–12.
🔄
Opening repertoire review: Replay 5 opening sequences you've actually played. Fix any moves that went wrong.
Discipline
After 6 weeks of games, your opening repertoire has real data. Reviewing where you deviated from your plan and what the engine recommends converts your actual game database into personalised opening prep.
📊 Output: 1–2 specific opening improvements noted. Repertoire updated. Comfortable in your system for ≥80% of games.

WK 09
Advanced Tactics: Double Attacks & Discovered Checks
These two motifs separate 1000 players from 1200 players. Master them here.
▶
⚡
Puzzle theme sprint: 20 discovered attack puzzles + 20 double attack puzzles. 3 days of themed drilling.
Passive Learning
Discovered attacks and double attacks are the "combination starters" that lead to forced wins in the 900–1200 range. They almost always involve a surprise — only pattern recognition makes them reliably visible before it's too late.
📊 Output: Discovered attack accuracy ≥78%. Double attack accuracy ≥80%. Can explain the mechanism of each without a board.
🔢
3-move combination drill: 10 positions daily where the solution is exactly 3 moves. No hints. Time yourself: max 3 min per position.
Skill Gap
Three-move combinations are the ceiling for most below-1200 games. At 1200, you need to be able to execute them under time pressure reliably. Timed drilling simulates game conditions far better than untimed puzzles.
📊 Output: Average solve time <2 min per 3-mover. Accuracy ≥65%. Progress tracked over 4 weeks.
📈
Rating push week: Play 8 rated games with specific intent to execute tactics you've drilled. No passive games.
Plateau
Weeks 9–12 are the conversion phase — converting training into rating. Active games (where you consciously look for drilled patterns) accelerate transfer from training to competition performance.
📊 Output: Rating delta this week logged. At least 1 win attributed directly to a drilled tactic (note it).
🛡️
Pre-game mental routine: Write a 3-step pre-game checklist (e.g. "breathe, blunder-check, candidate moves"). Use it before every game.
Tilt
A consistent pre-game ritual reduces anxiety and activates the analytical mindset. This is standard sports psychology — a written, repeated routine signals to your brain that it's time to calculate, not react emotionally.
📊 Output: Routine written and printed/saved. Applied before every game in weeks 9–12. Track if tilt rate decreases.

WK 11
Peak Rating Push — Maximum Game Volume
This is the highest-intensity week. Training is secondary. Games and analysis are primary.
▶
🔥
Play 12–14 rated rapid games this week. Full habit stack active. No skipping analysis after losses.
Discipline
Week 11 is the volume peak. After 10 weeks of skill-building, you need sufficient game data for the rating system to reflect your true strength. More games = more accurate rating. Do not hold back.
📊 Output: 12+ games completed. Rating at highest point yet. If under 1100, identify the last remaining bottleneck immediately.
🎯
Tactics maintenance: 20 puzzles/day (mixed). Do not drop below 80% accuracy. If you do, return to pure thematic drilling.
Plateau
Tactical sharpness must be maintained under high game volume. Twenty mixed puzzles daily takes <25 minutes and ensures pattern recognition stays active even when game analysis dominates the session.
📊 Output: 7 consecutive days at ≥80% accuracy. No thematic regression required (if regression occurs, pause games and re-drill for 2 days).
🧩
Post-game protocol enforced for every game: 5 mins, identify the losing move, record. Do not skip even one.
Passive Learning
At 12–14 games per week, skipping analysis becomes tempting. Enforcing the 5-minute post-game protocol, even if brief, keeps the feedback loop intact during the highest-volume week.
📊 Output: 100% post-game protocol completion rate. Losing move log has 12+ entries. Patterns becoming more specific.
🧘
Tilt protocol: Mandatory 1-hour break after any 3-game losing streak. No exceptions. Log every application.
Tilt
High-volume weeks are peak tilt risk. Extending the standard 2-loss rule to a 3-loss rule with a longer 1-hour break acknowledges that fatigue compounds emotional decision-making in extended sessions.
📊 Output: Tilt rule applied 100% of the time. Log compliance rate. Games played after enforced break — did it help?
